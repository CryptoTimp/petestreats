Welcometo Petes Treatzzz

After a few meals where no more pressure was added to an already stretched NHS and mates saying they'd eat it again if it was free, I decided I was quite enjoying cooking and baking during lockdown. I created this food page with a view to exploring as many different dishes as possible and carving out a new career path for myself. As a relative newcomer to 'proper cooking' the learning process has involved borrowing a lot of recipes from different sources. The recipes for each dish are therefore adapted and I've provided links to both the original recipe and my own notes on what I've found after cooking them. Bon App!

PetesCuisine

Rhubarb & Custard Cake

This cake is a delicious matchup of the sweet and sour flavours of Rhubarb and Custard and perfect for when Rhubarb starts coming into season (around February/March in the UK).

Vegan Chicken Kiev

This recipe can take a little while to prepare but it’s worth the wait.

Chocolate Brownies

These brownies are absolutely delicious and a sprinkle of cocoa powder over the top gives them a real wow factor when serving. One thing I found: make sure you melt the butter and chocolate before starting steps 5, 6 and 7 otherwise you could be in for a loooooonnngggg waaaaiiiitttt -.- while it cools zzzzzz

Vegetarian Beef Wellington

A great winter warmer that looks the part! This recipe is one that can have ingredients added to it or substituted. Feel free to add a few more herbs like thyme or bay leaves or add a bit of swede to the mixture.

Vegan Lemon Cheesecake

I know you’ll probably flinch when you see how much coconut yogurt you’ll need to buy but I genuinely think the taste is better with coconut yogurt than other alternatives such as blended cashew nuts. It’s also surprisingly simple to make after you’ve strained the yogurt overnight.

Falafels served with Aloo Gobi

I know, I know, falafels without chickpeas but they basically taste the same and I mean what else would you call them? Even if you came up with a name you’d probably just end up calling them falafels… the falafels flavour is twice as good the following day so you’d be well advised to make them a day in advance.

Chocolate Chip Cookies

If your ovens like mine you’ll need to be on it supervising the cookies to make sure they don’t burn/go too hard. Don’t worry about taking them out when they’re soft in the middle because they’ll continue to harden on the cooling rack.

Courgetti with Red Pesto

Easy to make and worth the effort of making your own pesto for the flavour. Add Chilli flakes to the pesto for an extra kick. Make sure you don’t over do the courgetti in the pan, keep it al dente and don’t forget it still cook a bit more with the pesto when warmed through.
